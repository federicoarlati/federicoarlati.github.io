Research

Current Position

Pre-doctoral Researcher – IGIER, Bocconi University

This project studies how automation and artificial intelligence affect firms and workers in France. We use linked administrative data from DADS, FARE, and LIFI, which connect employees to firms and provide information on wages, occupations, and balance sheets. We attempt to examine how technology adoption affects the composition of jobs inside firms, the wage distribution, and productivity. (Sauvagnat J.)

Dondena

Research Assistant – Dondena Centre, Bocconi University

At the Dondena Centre, several projects study how social environment and information shape opportunities early in life.

“Shall I Trust You? Diversity, Trust, and Cheating Behavior in School Children” uses a randomized field experiment with middle-school students in Milan. It combines behavioral games with school information to study how diversity affects trust, honesty, and educational aspirations. (Casarico A., Ghisolfi S., Marcolongo G., Serra C.)

“Trust in a Diverse World” uses experimental treatments that vary the gender, disability, and ethnicity of partners to study how stereotypes affect economic decisions. (Casarico A., Balbo N., Serra C.)

“Gender Inequality over the Life Cycle, Information Provision and Policy Preferences” analyzes open-ended survey responses using NLP methods to study how people describe discrimination and the motherhood penalty. (Casarico A., Schuetz J., Uebelmesser S.)
